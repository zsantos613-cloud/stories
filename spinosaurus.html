One of the largest known theropod dinosaurs alongside Tyrannosaurus and Giganotosaurus. The Spinosaurus stood out due to its uniquely adapted semi-aquatic life style. It had a long crocodile-like snout and conical teeth used for catching and gripping fish. The body of the Spinosaurus also had a wide paddle-like tail allowing it to easily navigate aquatic environments. It's most iconic feature is its tall sail alongside its back which it may have used for thermoregulation.
