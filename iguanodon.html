One of the earliest dinosaurs to ever be discovered and formally named was in fact the Iguanodon. Like many other dinosaurs its depiction has changed over centuries of time, its iconic thumb spike that it used to defend itself from predators, was actually thought to be a nasal horn. The Iguanodon is also a good example of facultatove bipedalism as it could function on its back hindlegs or all four of them.
